mars mission timeline

  • June 10, 2003

    Spirit Launches

    NASA's Spirit rover—and it's twin Opportunity—studied the history of climate and water at sites on Mars where conditions may once have been favorable to life.

  • july 8, 2003

    Opportunity Launches

    NASA's Opportunity rover was one of the most successful and enduring interplanetary missions.

  • May 25, 2011

    Spirit's mission declared complete.

    NASA ended efforts to contact the marooned rover and declared its mission complete. The rover had been silent since March 2010.

  • Nov. 26, 2011

    Curiosity Launches

    Curiosity's ongoing mission is to study the ancient habitability and the potential for life on Mars.

  • July 30, 2020

    Perseverance Launches.

    The Perseverance rover's astrobiology mission is to seek out signs of past microscopic life on Mars, explore the diverse geology of its landing site, Jezero Crater, and demonstrate key technologies that will help us prepare for future robotic and human exploration.

  • Feb. 18, 2021

    Expected Landing for Perseverance on Mars

    "Perseverance is the most capable rover in history because it is standing on the shoulders of our pioneers Sojourner, Spirit, Opportunity, and Curiosity," said Michael Watkins, director of NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory in Southern California. "In the same way, the descendants of Ingenuity and MOXIE will become valuable tools for future explorers to the Red Planet and beyond."
